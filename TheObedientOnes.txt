Tedium, anyhow, was part of a life of honest feelings.

They were obedient. Not just out of submissiveness: as in a sonnet, it was obedience from love of symmetry. Symmetry was their possible art.

The husband, influenced by the millieu of afflicted masculinity in which he lived, and by his own as well, which was shy but effective, started to think that life would be many love affairs.
